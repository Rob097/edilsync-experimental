# The "small detail" that wasn't small - how do you avoid this?

**Platform:** Reddit

**Subreddit:** r/homeowners

**Scheduled:** December 18, 2025 at 09:00 AM

My cousin just went through a kitchen reno and this drove her crazy.

She spent weeks picking out every detail. Cabinet hardware in brushed nickel. Specific tile. Particular grout color. Discussed everything with the contractor.

Halfway through, she visits the site. The tile guy installed chrome trim instead of brushed nickel.

His reason? "That's what was in stock, and it's basically the same thing."

The contractor's response? "You never specified the exact finish in writing, so we went with what was available."

She had to pay extra to redo it. Then the same thing happened with the cabinet hinges.

Here's what I don't get - she DID discuss all this. The contractor nodded and took notes. But somewhere between the GC and the tile installer, the information just... evaporated.

How are you supposed to prevent this? Write down every single micro-decision in a contract? That seems insane.
