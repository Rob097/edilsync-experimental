During my surveying studies I observed something that stuck with me.

Contractor and homeowner had a huge dispute over "skim coat." The contractor mentioned during the walkthrough that they'd "skim coat where needed." The homeowner nodded.

Three weeks later, contractor presents a €4,500 charge for skim coating the entire house - walls and ceilings.

Homeowner loses it. "I never authorized the whole house!"

Contractor: "Skim coat means full coverage for quality work. That's standard."

Both were technically right. But "where needed" meant completely different things to each of them.

The dispute dragged on for weeks and nearly went to arbitration.

For contractors - how do you avoid this? Do you define every term explicitly? Write everything in plain language? I'm genuinely curious how you prevent these communication explosions before they happen.
