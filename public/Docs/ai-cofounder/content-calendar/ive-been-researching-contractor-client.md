I've been researching contractor-client communication for a while now, and something keeps coming up that surprises me.

Contractors tell me they take photos of everything, send updates via text, get change orders signed, keep detailed records... and they STILL end up in disputes.

One guy told me he had a rock-solid contract, documented every conversation, and still had 4 nightmare clients this year who found ways to argue about scope and costs.

It made me realize the problem isn't lack of documentation. It's that all this documentation is scattered. Photos in one place, texts in another, signed change orders in a folder somewhere, verbal conversations that never got written down.

When a dispute happens, nobody can quickly see the full story. Both sides have "proof" but it's all fragmented.

I'm curious - for those of you who document heavily, do you find it actually prevents disputes? Or does it just give you ammunition when disputes happen anyway?
