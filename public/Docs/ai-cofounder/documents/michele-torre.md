

**Michele Torre, 42**
Owner, residential remodeling contractor

> "I don't have time to learn another tool that my guys won't use. I need something that just works and keeps my clients from calling me ten times a day."

## Traits
- Practical
- Skeptical
- Hands-on
- Detail-oriented
- Reputation-conscious
- Time-starved

## Their story
Michele runs a residential remodeling company in the suburbs, handling 8-12 kitchen and bathroom projects per year with his crew of 3. He's built his business entirely on referrals and positive reviews—every detail matters because one bad Yelp review can cost him thousands. He wakes up at 5:30am to review the day's schedule, spends mornings on jobsites, and evenings chasing down subcontractors, responding to homeowner texts, and updating spreadsheets. His wife handles the books part-time. He's tried project management software twice before but abandoned both within weeks—too complicated, his crew wouldn't use them, and they didn't solve his real problem: keeping homeowners informed without endless phone calls and preventing those nightmare 'I thought you said...' disputes over change orders.

## Goals
- Complete projects on time and on budget without constant firefighting
- Protect and grow his reputation through satisfied clients and referrals
- Reduce evening/weekend work so he can have time with family
- Avoid payment disputes and get paid faster for completed work
- Streamline communication so homeowners feel informed without him being on call 24/7

## Pain points
- Spends 2+ hours daily answering the same homeowner questions via calls, texts, and emails
- Change order disputes erode profit margins and damage client relationships
- No single source of truth—project details scattered across texts, emails, photos, and paper
- Crew members don't check or respond to messages, creating communication bottlenecks
- Previous software was too complex, expensive, or didn't solve the real communication problem
- Late payments and scope creep cut into already-thin margins

## Objections
- I've tried project management software before and wasted money—why would this be different?
- My crew won't use another app. They barely check their phones for work stuff.
- I can't afford to spend weeks learning a new system or training my team.
- What if homeowners use this to micromanage me or dispute every little thing?
- The monthly cost might not justify the ROI if my margins are already thin.
