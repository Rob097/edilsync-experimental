## Design Context

### Users
EdilSync serves construction companies and site managers first on the public site. They need to understand quickly how the product helps them keep projects aligned, reduce wasted trips, document decisions, and protect margins without adding process overhead. Secondary audiences include clients, technical professionals, and subcontractors who enter the platform within the project context.

### Brand Personality
Practical, reliable, and field-aware. The interface should feel grounded in real construction work rather than generic SaaS language. It should communicate trust, operational clarity, and accountability, using familiar construction vocabulary and avoiding hype.

### Aesthetic Direction
Keep the current visual direction as the base, but sharpen it. The public site should feel clean and professional with an industrial-leaning polish, strong hierarchy, warm construction-adjacent accents, and restrained motion. Favor light mode, readable layouts, and surfaces that feel intentional rather than flashy.

### Design Principles
- Show operational value quickly: fewer calls, fewer misunderstandings, more control over time, cost, and accountability.
- Use familiar construction language before software language.
- Keep the visual tone trustworthy and practical, with enough distinction to feel designed rather than templated.
- Make navigation and page structure easy to scan on mobile and desktop.
- Use motion and decoration only where they clarify hierarchy or add confidence.
